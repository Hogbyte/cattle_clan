GENERAL INFORMATION:

Project Name: David's Marker Cases
Project Status: Completed!
Timeframe: November 2003 - March 2004


DESCRIPTION:

	Marker cases sold at retail stores are usually too expensive or they
lack personality.  So I decided to make my own out of some cheap toolboxes.


BUILD LOG:

Early November:
	I bought a Black & Decker toolbox at Wal Mart for under ten dollars.
I also bought gloss white Fusion spray paint.  I cleaned the toolbox and gave
it three coats of white paint.  I still had some universal black automotive
touch-up paint left from my Stringray project, so after allowing the paint to
dry, I used the touch-up paint to free-hand some cow spots.

Mid December:
	Painting cow spots with touch-up paint is fine if you're painting a
paintball marker, but it does not work when painting a toolbox.  I reluctantly
opened my wallet and bought a half-pint of Rust-oleum gloss black paint and
a few 50 cent disposable paint brushes.

Late February:
	After a month of no progress, I remembered my marker case project.  I
decided that I chose the wrong paint scheme.  I wanted flames on my case.  So
I took another trip to Wal Mart and bought a second Black & Decker toolbox,
painter's tape, a hobby knife, and a spray can of Krylon yellow orange
fluorescent paint (it was the only one that matched the orange on the toolbox).
	While at college, I cleaned the toolbox and covered it with painter's
tape.  I then drew my design on the tape, made a few modifications, and then
cut out the design with the hobby knife.
	When I got home that weekend I painted the toolbox with the orange paint.
After the paint dried, I removed the painter's tape to reveal the flames.  Using
some acrylic paint that I bought at Wal Mart, I painted a skull design.  The next
week I applied a coat of Minwax clear gloss polyurethane to each case.

Early March:
	Began applying the second coat of polyurethane.

March 9:
	Project complete!
